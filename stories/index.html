In Practice

I don’t think of these pages as a portfolio.

They don’t describe the projects I’m most proud of, nor do they try to represent everything I’ve done.

I collected them because each of them taught me something.

They are moments when a project changed the way I observed a system, organized information, or approached a problem.

People change.

Contexts change.

Technologies change.

The ideas that remain change much less.

These stories come from experiences in research, public administration, industry, the non-profit sector and, more recently, from observing digital products and services.

They aren’t arranged chronologically.

Instead, they follow the path through which my way of designing has evolved over the years.

Each story describes an experience.

Together, they describe a way of thinking.
