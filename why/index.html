Why I Keep Doing This

For a long time, I thought the part I enjoyed most was building something.

Over time, I realized that wasn’t really it.

The part that fascinates me begins earlier.

It’s the moment when I try to understand how a system works.

Observing it.

Asking questions.

Imagining a simpler, more elegant, or simply more natural way for its parts to work together.

It feels remarkably similar to what I experienced as a child building with LEGO.

Or to the feeling I still get today when solving a good puzzle.

That’s why I find it difficult to think of it as just work.

If no one ever asked me to do it again, I would probably keep doing it anyway.

During a walk.

While swimming.

Listening to music.

Or on a quiet Sunday with nothing planned.

That’s when the best ideas usually arrive.

When I stop looking for a solution and simply start playing with a question.

Looking back, I realize this has always been the thread running through everything I’ve done.

Not a technology.

Not a profession.

The pleasure of understanding how things work.

And the quiet satisfaction that comes when a solution suddenly feels so simple it seems as if it had been there all along.

The ideas that stay with me the longest are almost always the ones I end up playing with.
